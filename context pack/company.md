# company.md

**Company:** Terraloom

**Business model:** SaaS subscription — architecture and construction firms pay per seat (project managers, architects, field leads) to manage project handoffs, file sharing, and task tracking across design and build phases. Revenue is ~80% recurring subscription, ~20% professional services for enterprise onboarding.

**Stage:** Series B — scaling GTM into mid-market (150–500 seat firms), expanding from West Coast to national coverage.

**Current strategic focus:** Retention and expansion in the 150–300 seat tier. NRR is the north star metric this year. We are not investing in new feature categories — all engineering capacity goes to depth on existing features and reducing churn from known pain points.

**What we are NOT doing this year:**
- No new vertical expansion (we are staying in architecture and construction only)
- No consumer or SMB product motion (minimum viable firm size remains 50 seats)
- Mobile is a Q4 initiative — not being resourced in Q1–Q3
- No self-serve onboarding — all new customers go through a CSM-led onboarding flow
- No marketplace or integration platform — partner integrations are handled case by case

**Key metrics we care about:**
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) — target 115% by end of year
- Median time-to-value for new customers (target: first successful project handoff within 21 days of contract)
- Monthly active seat ratio — how many contracted seats are active in a given month (current: 67%, target: 80%)

**Competitive position:** Terraloom wins on workflow specificity — we are built for the architecture-to-construction handoff, not adapted from generic project management tools. Our closest competitors (Procore, PlanGrid) are broader and more complex. We win on ease of adoption and on the specific pain of cross-discipline file handoffs.
